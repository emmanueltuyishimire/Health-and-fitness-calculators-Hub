Workout Calorie Tracker

Estimate the number of calories burned during your workout. This tool uses MET values to provide a scientific estimate of your energy expenditure, helping you better understand your overall TDEE.

How to Use the Workout Calorie Tracker

This calculator estimates the calories you burn during a specific workout based on its intensity (MET value), your body weight, and the duration.

  1. Enter Your Weight: Your body weight is a key factor in how many calories you burn.
  2. Select Your Activity: Use the searchable dropdown to find your workout. Each one has a pre-assigned MET value.
  3. Enter the Duration: Input how many minutes you performed the workout.
  4. Calculate Your Burn: The tool will show you the estimated total calories burned for that session.

Understanding METs and Calorie Expenditure

What is a MET?

MET stands for Metabolic Equivalent of Task. It's a standardized measure used to express the energy cost of physical activities. One MET is defined as the amount of oxygen consumed while sitting at rest, which is approximately 3.5 milliliters of oxygen per kilogram of body weight per minute. In simpler terms:

  • 1 MET: The energy you use when you are at complete rest. This is your BMR.
  • 6 METs: The activity requires six times the energy of resting.

The MET value is a convenient way to compare the intensity of different activities. For example, light weight lifting has a MET of 3.0, while vigorous lifting has a MET of 6.0.

The Calculation Formula

This calculator uses a standard scientific formula to estimate calorie burn:

Total Calories Burned = Duration (min) × [(MET × 3.5 × Weight (kg)) / 200]

This formula accurately converts the oxygen consumption represented by the MET value into the calories burned for a person of a specific weight over a specific time. While it's a powerful estimation, remember that individual factors like age, gender, and body composition can cause slight variations.

Common Mistakes
  • Overestimating Intensity: Choosing "vigorous" weight lifting for a session with long rest periods and low effort will overestimate your calorie burn. Be honest with your intensity.
  • Including Rest Time: The duration should only be the time you were actively performing the exercise, not the total time spent in the gym.
  • "Eating Back" Workout Calories: Your exercise is already factored into your TDEE if you selected an appropriate activity level. Use this tool for information, not as a license to eat more.
  • Ignoring the "Afterburn" Effect: Intense workouts, especially resistance training, can slightly elevate your metabolism for hours afterward (EPOC). This calculator does not account for that, so the total burn may be slightly higher.
Pro Tips & Quick Hacks
  • Prioritize Compound Lifts: Exercises like squats, deadlifts, and bench presses use multiple muscle groups and burn more calories than isolation exercises.
  • Increase Density: To burn more calories in the same amount of time, reduce your rest periods between sets or use supersets.
  • Focus on NEAT: Remember that calories burned outside of your workout (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis) are also a huge part of your TDEE. Track your daily steps with the Steps to Calories Calculator.
  • Fuel Your Workouts: To perform well and burn more calories, ensure you're adequately fueled. Use the Macronutrient Ratio Calculator to plan your pre-workout nutrition.
